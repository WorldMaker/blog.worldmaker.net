I was skimming old blog articles and realized it
has been a while since I've done small posts. Some
quick updates:

- I separated from my job in April, so I have my
  shingle out and am now looking for new work.
  Maybe [given my feelings on recent "AI" trends][trends]
  I'm looking for a new career.
- I procrastinated my job search by spending a
  week cleaning my house and then hosting my first
  [Thunder] party in too long.
- I procrastinated my job search by having a lot
  of fun building new features for [my book club's site][bookclub].
  Maybe I'm not entirely done with my career, I
  just need someone to trust my creativity. Also,
  I had to migrate the site to a different backend.
  Tech debt touches everything.
- Speaking of tech debt, I procrastinated my job
  search yesterday by migrating my entire blog
  from Jekyll to [Lume]. I was tired of Ruby
  gem Dependabot warnings that never felt like
  they mattered to me, and I was tired of a
  preview experience that required a lot of WSL
  shenanigans. I've become a big fan of Lume as
  a very powerful static site generator. (I'm
  doing some pretty complex fun things in that
  Book Club site.) It's nice to have good Tags
  pages again.
- [Portless] has some Windows quirks but is
  handy enough to forgive them.
- News of my separation from my job reached me
  in the middle of my vacation. It feels weird
  that has [happened twice] now. I know it is
  just business, but it felt deeply personal,
  especially given an end date that coincided with
  my birthday weekend. I spent the first half of
  my vacation having an incredible time at
  Disneyland. Disneyland kept asking me questions
  about if I have enough creativity in my life.
  I kept asking questions why Disney doesn't have
  enough creativity in its apps and if I could
  find a way to explore that professionally. I
  think I was going to be looking for new
  challenges this year even if my job hadn't
  forced me to consider it the hard way between
  an incredible week at Disneyland and a lovely
  week on JoCoCruise 2026, both with a lot of
  great friends doing awesome things.
